Programming

When I was only 10 years old, I created my very first website with HTML, CSS and javascript - a website about cupcakes! It was glorious with tons of pictures and recipes for all different kinds of cupcakes. Like cupcakes, computer programming has been a consistent love of mine for a long time.

I was always mathematically inclined - math always felt logical and puzzle-like, while English was often difficult (especially as a non-native English speaker when I was younger - with immigrant family members who spoke almost no English). I love the puzzle-like nature of computer science and the feeling when you've created something (with no bugs!).

I didn't realize that being in computer science was a possible career route until my junior year of high school. I entered a program called "Girls Who Code" that changed my life forever. Girls Who Code taught me basic computer science foundations, leadership skills and most importantly, that I could be a Software Engineer if I put my mind to it.

From then on, I pursued my passion like a mouse chasing a ball of cheese. I joined many different computer science organizations such as CodeNow and #BuiltByGirls, competed in hackathons like the TechCrunch Hackathon and NY SpaceApps, and participated in competitions such as the #BuiltByGirls Challenge. I am now a sophomore studying Computer Science at Smith College that is working on software, machine learning, image processsing and more.
